📘 How to Read Whale Data

Whale trades are large, high-volume options trades made by institutions or big players. Tracking these trades can give insight into future market moves — especially when they cluster, repeat, or aggressively favor one direction.

🔍 Why Some Fields Might Be Empty

  • No symbols appear: The whale feed may be temporarily empty. Try refreshing later.
  • Can't select Call or Put: Only one type (e.g., Calls) may exist for a symbol at the time.
  • Empty Strike or Expiration: The data may be missing matching strike/expiry pairs.

This is normal — it’s part of learning how to read live option flow.

📘 What the Fields Mean

  • Symbol: The stock ticker being traded (e.g., AAPL, NVDA).
  • Type: Call or Put — calls are bullish bets, puts are bearish.
  • Strike: The price the option controls. It’s where the trader believes the stock may move.
  • Expiration: When the option contract ends (weekly, monthly, etc.).
  • Premium: How much was paid for the contract — higher = more conviction.
  • Open Interest (OI): How many open contracts exist. Spikes = building interest.

🎯 Interpreting the Whale Meter

The Whale Meter on the homepage shows total premium traded in real time. As it fills up and changes color, it signals intensity:

  • Blue: Quiet market
  • Aqua: Normal flow
  • Orange: Elevated activity
  • Red: 🚨 Whale-level aggression
