📘 Calls vs. Puts – In Plain English

If you’re new to options trading, the words “call” and “put” can sound like secret code. This guide explains what they really mean—without the jargon.

💵 What’s a Put?

A put is a way to get paid to wait for a discount.

Let’s say you like a stock, but you only want to buy it if it drops to $100.
You agree: “If it drops to $100, I’ll buy it.”
In return, someone pays you cash right now for that promise.

  • If the stock doesn’t drop → you keep the cash ✅
  • If it does → you buy it at the discount you wanted ✅

This is called “selling a put,” but think of it like:
“I’ll buy your stock if it gets cheaper—pay me now just in case.”

📦 What’s a Call?

A call is a way to get paid while waiting to sell.

You already own a stock. You’re willing to sell it if it hits $120.
So you say: “If it hits $120, I’ll sell it.”
Someone pays you now for that agreement.

  • If it never hits $120 → you keep your stock and the cash ✅
  • If it does → you sell at a profit—and still keep the cash ✅

This is called “selling a call,” but it really means:
“I’ll sell my stock later—pay me now to lock in the price.”
